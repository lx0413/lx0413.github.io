Xiang LI: Teaching

Courses as Independent Instructor

As a sole instructor at the University of Oregon, I work closely with students to help them achieve personal academic goals and organize graduate employee grading. Entering Spring 2021, I have been the sole instructor a total of 6 times in 2 distinct undergraduate-level courses, in person and online. I have taught classes with enrollment ranging from small groups of 20 students to large classes of 85 students. My course materials written in R markdown are shared in my Github repos, including syllabus, lecture slides, practice problems, and study guide.

  • EC370 Money and Banking: Summer 2018, Winter 2019, Fall 2019, Spring 2020 (remote), Spring 2021 (remote)

    • Github repo

  • EC311 Intermediate Macroeconomic Theory: Summer 2019

    • Github repo

Courses as Lab Teaching Assistant

Entering Fall 2020, I have assisted in teaching 2 undergraduate-level classes a total of 4 times. Enrollment ranges from small groups of 7 students to larger groups of 48 students. As a lab teaching assistant, I teach students how to use Stata, R and Rstudio to analyze data and identify causal relationships. I also lead discussion and organize group activities.

  • EC320 Introduction to Econometrics: Spring 2019, Fall 2020

  • EC202 Intro to Economic Analysis: Macroeconomics: Spring 2017, Winter 2018
